## 1. Marketing Psychology Principles to Bake In

These aren't tactics — they're the frame everything else sits inside. Apply them consistently.

### 1.1 Identity Shift (the real product)

People don't buy a course. They buy a version of themselves.

The Cipher Commons buyer is a finance professional, accountant, or IT manager who knows they're underprepared for fraud, cyber threats, or automation — and feels quietly behind. The product is not "GFACT prep". The product is: **you, but credible and confident in a space that used to intimidate you.**

Every piece of copy should speak to the identity they want, not the content you're selling.

> **Wrong**: "Six modules covering GFACT exam domains"
> **Right**: "The month you stopped feeling out of your depth on cyber"

---

### 1.2 Specificity = Trust

Vague claims kill conversion. Specific claims build credibility.

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| "Learn about fraud investigation" | "By module 3, you'll read a balance sheet looking for the same red flags a forensic accountant does" |
| "AI tools for professionals" | "We'll build an automated client report in 40 minutes using Claude — no code" |
| "Experts in the field" | "Lynsey holds GIAC GSEC and GFACT — two of the hardest certs in cyber and forensic accounting" |

Use numbers, time-to-result, and named credentials everywhere.

---

### 1.3 Social Proof Architecture

Structure proof in layers — don't wait until you have testimonials.

| Stage | Proof available | How to use it |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|
| Pre-launch | Waitlist size, waitlist names/job titles | "143 professionals already on the list" |
| Month 1–2 | Email replies, DMs, LinkedIn comments | Screenshot and share (with permission) |
| Month 3+ | Member wins — cert passes, first case, first AI workflow | Weekly win posts in community + LinkedIn |
| Month 6+ | Cohort outcomes, employer referrals, case studies | Full case study + video testimonial |

**Never fake scarcity. Use real scarcity.** Founding member pricing has a deadline — use a hard close date, not "closing soon."

---

### 1.4 Fear of Being Left Behind (FOMO of competence)

The audience's core fear is professional irrelevance — not "missing a deal."

LinkedIn posts, email subject lines, and landing page copy should touch this nerve lightly but consistently:

> "The fraud examiner your firm brings in next year will have skills most accountants have never heard of."

> "Cyber insurance premiums are rising. The consultants who can explain why are the ones getting the calls."

---

### 1.5 Anchoring

Set the £149/mo Pro price in context before you mention £79.

> "A half-day forensic accounting course from a Big Four firm typically runs £1,200–2,000. The GFACT exam alone is £400. Cipher Commons Pro is £149/mo."

The cohort at £1,995 anchors the membership tiers as the obvious "sensible" option.

---

### 1.6 Commitment & Consistency (the 5-day challenge mechanism)

The email challenge works because of psychology, not content.

- Day 1: small action (answer one question) → creates commitment
- Day 2–4: micro-wins → investment builds
- Day 5: offer → person who has done 5 days is far more likely to buy than cold traffic

**The challenge is a foot-in-the-door, not a content dump.** Each email should be under 300 words. One idea. One action. One link.

## 2. Marketing Ideas by Channel

### 2.1 LinkedIn (Primary — Paul + Lynsey)

**Posting cadence**: 3×/week each, staggered. Not the same day.

**Content pillars**:

| Pillar | What it looks like | Frequency |
|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| 🔐 Expertise signal | "Here's what I actually look for when reviewing a client's cyber posture" | 1×/week |
| 📊 Industry data | "Fraud losses in UK SMEs were £X in 2024 — here's the pattern" | 1×/week |
| 💬 Community wins | Member passed GSEC / built their first workflow / caught a red flag | 1×/week |
| 🎯 Direct CTA | "Founding membership closes [date]. Link in bio." | 1×/month max |

**Lynsey's angle**: forensic accounting is almost invisible on LinkedIn. That's an advantage. Posts about fraud patterns, investigation workflow, GFACT vs ACFE comparisons will get traction in a near-empty space.

**Paul's angle**: IT security for SMEs, Cyber Essentials war stories, AI tools that actually work. Bridges the Bluewater Associates brand into Cipher Commons.

**Format that works**:
- No-image text posts with a hard first line (scroll-stopper)
- Carousel PDFs for "5 things" content
- 30–60 second video (phone quality is fine — rehearsed but not scripted)
- Poll → follow-up post with results
